Frappuccino

Cappuccino

This particular recipe is a drink for the people who love to keep the brain sharp. If you don’t like black coffee, Cappuccino is here for the rescue. Is an espresso-based coffee drink that originated in Italy. Variations of the drink involve the use of cream instead of milk, using non-dairy milks.

Espresso

Espresso is a dark, concentrated liquid that is “pulled”, with enormous pressure and skill, through powerful, purpose-built machines. In comparison to coffees brewed without pressure, the drink produced by an espresso machine is much darker.

Macchiato

The caffè macchiato has the highest ratio of espresso to milk of any drink made with those ingredients. Caffè macchiato, sometimes called espresso macchiato, is an espresso coffee drink with a small amount of milk, usually foamed.

Espresso con panna

Treat yourself to the quality of a professional cup coffee. Espresso meets a dollop of whipped cream to enhance the rich and caramelly flavors of a straight-up shot. Espresso con panna, which means "espresso with cream" in Italian.

Mocha

A caffè mocha, also called mocaccino, is a chocolate-flavoured variant of a caffè latte. Other commonly used spellings are mochaccino and also mochachino. The name is derived from the city of Mocha, Yemen, which was one of the centers of early coffee trade.

Caffe Latte

The unmistakable taste and aroma that we all know and love is still as good as ever. Our signature coffee has a medium-dark roast that gives it a full flavour and wonderfully invigorating taste. The word comes from the Italian caffè e latte, caffelatte, which means "coffee & milk"

Frappe

A frappé coffee, Greek frappé, Nescafé frappé, or just frappé is a Greek iced coffee drink made from instant coffee, water, sugar, and milk. The word is often written frappe. The frappé was invented through experimentation by Dimitris Vakondios, a Nescafe representative, in 1957 in Thessaloniki.
